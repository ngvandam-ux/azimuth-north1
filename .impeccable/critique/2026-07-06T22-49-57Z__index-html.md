## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** Not AI slop. No gradient text, no side-stripe card borders as decoration, no glassmorphism-as-decoration, no fake hero-metric row, no identical icon-card grids. Palette discipline holds — only the documented ink/paper/brass/oxblood/mint tokens appear (plus one undocumented recurring shade, see below). The hero terrain canvas is confirmed the only ambient motion, and `prefers-reduced-motion` is respected wherever motion exists.

The "Plate I–VI" numbered-eyebrow device is earned — it's a genuine continuous sequence spanning index.html through about.html, not a per-page "01/02/03" reset. However, the page runs **four concurrent numbering systems at once** (Plates I–VI, capability indices i–iv, process stages I–V, charter articles i–iii). Individually well-crafted; stacked, it risks reading as numbering-on-numbering — the one place discipline tips into over-design.

**Deterministic scan:** `detect.mjs --json` on all 5 HTML files: exit code 2, 55 findings (all warning/advisory, none error) — `design-system-color`×34, `layout-transition`×6, `overused-font`×5, `bounce-easing`×4, `em-dash-overuse`×4, `single-font`×2.

Manual verification found the detector is mostly right but imprecise:
- `single-font` on both article pages is a **false positive** — they load and use 3 fonts via `assets/reader.css`; the detector didn't parse the external stylesheet.
- `bounce-easing` **undercounts** — 6 real occurrences of `cubic-bezier(.34,1.56,.64,1)` exist (brand-hover rotate, rose-needle rotate, button arrows), detector caught only 4.
- The 34 `design-system-color` hits are almost entirely **one recurring undocumented shade** (`#efe6d0`, i.e. `rgba(239,230,208,*)`) used consistently site-wide (compass-rose SVG accents, footer/nav text) — a real missing design token, not 34 unrelated problems.
- `backdrop-filter` (3 hits, scrolled-nav blur) and `border-left`-as-accent (3 hits, pull-quote/blockquote styling) are both **defensible, on-brand uses**, not the generic anti-patterns those greps target.

No visual overlay was injected into a human-visible browser tab for this run (the harness-specific live-server/injection flow wasn't used inside an isolated sub-agent); evidence instead came from a self-contained Playwright probe against the live production site, disclosed here rather than claimed as an in-browser overlay.

## Overall Impression

This is a genuinely disciplined, non-generic marketing site — the palette, motion restraint, and copy voice all hold up under scrutiny, and the contrast/typography fundamentals are real strengths, not lucky defaults. But two independent assessments converged on the same root problem from different angles: **the responsive nav breaks in a way that can make the entire header — including the primary "Book a Call" action — unreachable on common tablet widths**, and **the whole homepage pitch is invisible if JavaScript is blocked or slow to load**. Those two issues matter more than anything else found here, because they can silently prevent the site's one job (getting a visitor to book a call) from being possible at all.

## What's Working

- **Contrast discipline is real and verified, not assumed.** Computed live: body-on-paper ≈7.47:1, hero-copy-on-ink ≈7.98:1, CTA button text ≈6.10:1 — all comfortably clear AA, and deliberately achieved rather than a lucky default.
- **The terrain canvas is the only ambient motion on the page, and it's respected everywhere `prefers-reduced-motion` matters.** Confirmed in both the CSS media query and the JS engine's own `matchMedia` gate.
- **The "Rip and replace" vs. "Fit to your workflow" comparison card and the Cal.com in-page booking modal** are both specific, on-brand, and functionally solid — zero console errors were found anywhere across all 5 pages.

## Priority Issues

**[P0] Nav breaks completely in a ~140px "dead zone" (761–899px), including on common tablet widths.** `index.html`/`about.html` gate nav-collapse at `max-width:760px`, while `assets/reader.css` (library + articles) and DESIGN.md's own documented spec use 900px. Between those two thresholds — which covers iPad-portrait's 768px CSS viewport — the nav links don't collapse but also don't fit: they wrap awkwardly into the wordmark, and the "Book a Call" CTA is measured rendering at `x:774.5–843`, entirely outside a 768px viewport with no scrollbar cue that it exists. Below 760px, only the CTA remains and every other nav link (including Library, About, Insights) disappears with no hamburger/drawer replacement anywhere in the DOM.
**Why it matters:** At a common tablet width, literally nothing in the header — not even the primary "Book a Call" action — is visible or reachable.
**Fix:** Ship a real mobile nav (hamburger + slide-out/sheet) below one consistent breakpoint (900px, matching `reader.css` and DESIGN.md), and delete the mismatched 760px rule from `index.html`/`about.html`.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable adapt`

**[P0] Homepage content is invisible with no JS fallback.** 37 elements carry `.rv` (`opacity:0; transform:translateY(34px)`), reversed only by a JS-added `.in` class; the hero's `.thesis .fade` block is the same pattern. No `<noscript>` exists anywhere.
**Why it matters:** If the reveal script is blocked, errors, or loads late on a slow connection, visitors see a blank page below the fold — the entire pitch.
**Fix:** Default all `.rv`/`.fade` content to visible; use JS only to add the animated-entrance state, never to gate baseline visibility.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable harden`

**[P1] Zero proof, pricing signal, or client names anywhere on the homepage.** Confirmed via full-text extraction — no testimonial, logo, or named outcome exists.
**Why it matters:** The stated target persona (a skeptical SMB owner) is asked to book a call on self-asserted principles alone, with no third-party reassurance.
**Fix:** Add at least one concrete named result or short testimonial near the final CTA.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable craft` (social-proof section)

**[P2] Brass eyebrow labels fail contrast on paper backgrounds.** Computed: `rgb(191,155,86)` (brass) on `rgb(242,239,230)` (paper) = 2.27:1, fails WCAG AA (needs 4.5:1). The sepia token (`#5c503c`, 6.85:1) already solves this correctly elsewhere in the system.
**Why it matters:** Small brass-on-paper labels (e.g. the "FIELD NOTE" tag) are hard to read for many users and fail the site's own stated WCAG 2.1 AA bar.
**Fix:** Swap brass-on-paper small-text instances to the sepia token already used elsewhere for this exact purpose.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable polish`

**[P2] Multiple touch targets fall well under the 44×44px guideline at mobile width.** Measured at 390px: nav brand/logo (185×30), nav "Book a Call" CTA (136×35), "Follow on LinkedIn →" (182×18), "Meet the team →" (134×22), both `mailto:info@azimuth-north.com` links (~190×15–16).
**Why it matters:** These are plain inline text links with no padding box, so their tap target equals their line-height — clearly failing accessibility guidance for mobile tap accuracy.
**Fix:** Add vertical padding (or increase line-height/hit-area) to bring every interactive text link to at least 44px tall on mobile.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable adapt`

**[P3] `library.html` skips a heading level (h1 → h3, no h2).** All other pages have clean h1→h2→h3 order.
**Fix:** Promote the card titles to h2, or insert a visually-hidden h2 section label.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable polish`

**[P3] One undocumented recurring color token (`#efe6d0`) is missing from DESIGN.md.** Used consistently and deliberately site-wide (compass-rose accents, footer/nav text at various opacities) but not captured as a named token.
**Fix:** Add it to DESIGN.md's Neutral color group (e.g. "Parchment-accent") so it stops flagging in future detector runs and stays intentional rather than drifted.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable document`

## Minor Observations

- Footer has no privacy/terms link and no social icons (LinkedIn is a mid-page text link only) — a small trust gap for a firm handling client data.
- "Insights" (homepage anchor) and "Library" (full page) are separate nav items pointing at overlapping content types — mildly ambiguous about which to click, and the homepage nav's 7 items exceed the ≤4-per-decision-point cognitive-load guideline (subpages correctly trim to 5).
- The `.thesis` diagram's accessible name comes from `aria-label` on a bare `<div>` with no ARIA role — likely honored by Chrome/Firefox but inconsistent across all screen readers.
- One tab stop (10th on the homepage) computed a fully transparent `outlineColor` despite `outline-style: solid` — worth a manual check to confirm it's not an invisible focus ring on that specific link.

## Questions to Consider

1. If the whole pitch disappears without JavaScript and the entire nav disappears at common tablet widths, was this ever opened on an actual phone or tablet before launch — or only ever reviewed on a laptop?
2. The brand promises "warm-authority" credibility, yet there's not one named client, metric, or dollar figure on the site — is that a deliberate "book first, prove later" bet, or is the proof section simply not built yet?
3. With four concurrent roman-numeral systems running at once, would a visitor five minutes after leaving remember which "III" you meant — or has the numbering motif started working against its own legibility?
