# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Owners and decision-makers at small and mid-size businesses (contractors, regional firms, operators) who suspect their processes, systems, or data could work harder, but are wary of generic SaaS platforms and hype-driven AI pitches. They arrive skeptical, comparing this firm against both "rip and replace" software vendors and traditional big-consulting firms. The job to be done on this site: build enough trust, clarity, and credibility in a few minutes to book a free consult call.

## Product Purpose

A marketing site for Azimuth North, a veteran-led consulting and AI-implementation firm serving small and mid-size businesses. It exists to convert skeptical SMB owners into booked calls by demonstrating operator credibility (the founder has run a business and worn the uniform), showing a disciplined methodology (the five-stage Map/Fit/Connect/Visualize/Sustain process), and proving substance over hype (a research library, real case-style writing, transparent charter around ownership and vendor-neutrality). Success = qualified consult bookings via Cal.com.

## Brand Personality

Disciplined · precise · warm-authority. The voice is a field-surveyor/cartographer metaphor layered over a veteran-operator sensibility: exacting, unhurried, quietly confident rather than salesy. It earns its authority through specificity (real methodology, real charter commitments) rather than adjectives. Warmth comes through candor ("we'll tell you honestly where AI can't help") and craft (the hand-drawn annotation marks, the engraved-plate section numbering), not through friendliness-for-its-own-sake.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI/SaaS cream template**: pastel warm-neutral backgrounds, rounded cards, gradient text, tiny uppercase eyebrows above every section, hero-metric clichés. This is the single biggest risk given the site's AI-adjacent subject matter.
- **Cold corporate consulting cliché**: navy suits, stock-photo handshakes, McKinsey-deck blandness that sounds impressive but says nothing specific.
- **Loud startup/gimmick**: overanimated, neon, "disruptive"-coded design that undercuts the disciplined, veteran-operator register.

## Design Principles

1. **Earn authority through specificity, not adjectives.** Methodology, charter commitments, and real numbers do the persuading — not superlatives.
2. **Restraint is the brand.** One accent motion idea (the terrain) done well beats five competing motion ideas. Cut before adding.
3. **Every visual metaphor must trace back to cartography/surveying.** Contour lines, bearings, plates, field notes — decoration is only earned if it's on-theme.
4. **Candor over persuasion.** Copy should read like someone willing to say "this won't work for you" — that's what separates this firm from a sales site.
5. **Craft in the details, not just the hero.** The compass rose, the engraved band, the plate numbering — small recurring motifs matter more than one big splashy moment.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. `prefers-reduced-motion` must be fully respected (already implemented for major animations). Given the buyer persona may skew less technical, prioritize plain-language copy, generous touch targets, and not gating any content or key actions behind hover-only or JS-only interactions.
